# Serving with Humility

## Greatness in service is found in selflessness

Service is one of the clearest ways the gospel is lived out. Jesus did not come to be served, but to serve, and He called His followers to do the same. Christian service should not be driven by ambition, recognition, or pride. It should be motivated by love and shaped by humility.

> "Whoever would be great among you must be your servant." <cite>Matthew 20:26</cite>

Humility in service means we are willing to do what is needed without demanding attention or praise. A person who serves with humility does not measure their value by how visible their work is. They are content to help, encourage, and support others in ways that may go unnoticed by the world.

This kind of service is often more meaningful than grand gestures. The small acts of care, patience, and kindness are often the ones that most clearly reflect the heart of Christ. A quiet word of encouragement, a helpful hand, a patient response, or an act of sacrifice can carry great spiritual weight.

### Markers of humble service

- Willingness to help without recognition
- Patience with others in difficult seasons
- Joy in meeting needs quietly
- Readiness to learn and receive correction

The Lord looks not only at what we do, but at the spirit in which we do it. Service that is offered with pride becomes hollow. Service offered with humility becomes a testimony of grace. Such service draws people closer to God because it reflects His character.

If we want to serve well, we must also be willing to learn. Humility allows us to receive correction, to grow, and to follow the leading of others when necessary. It keeps us from becoming self-important and reminds us that every gift we have is meant to bless others.
