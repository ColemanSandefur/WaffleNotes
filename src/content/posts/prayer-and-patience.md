# Prayer and Patience

## Learning to wait on God

Prayer is one of the clearest marks of a life that depends on God. It is the act of turning away from self-sufficiency and placing our needs, hopes, and fears in the hands of the Lord. Yet prayer is not only for urgent moments. It is also a way of training the heart to wait with trust.

> "Be still, and know that I am God." <cite>Psalm 46:10</cite>

Patience is often overlooked in modern life because everything seems to move quickly. We want answers immediately and outcomes without delay. But faith is not always measured by speed. Sometimes faith is measured by how well we endure, trust, and keep praying while waiting.

The Scriptures teach us that prayer and patience belong together. We pray because we believe God hears us, and we wait because we believe God works in His own time. This combination protects us from discouragement and keeps us from becoming impatient with God. The Lord may answer in a moment, or He may lead us through a season of growth first.

### What patience looks like

- Trusting God when no answer comes quickly
- Continuing in prayer without resentment
- Remaining faithful during delay
- Choosing humility over pressure

There are many reasons a believer may need patience. A difficult circumstance may require endurance. A ministry burden may require steady effort. A personal struggle may demand humility and long-suffering. In each of these moments, prayer keeps the heart aligned with the Lord, while patience helps us remain faithful rather than frantic.

When we pray without patience, we may become discouraged by silence. When we wait without prayer, we may become proud and self-reliant. But when prayer and patience are held together, they produce maturity. We begin to see that God is not only answering our requests, but also shaping us through the process.

This is why prayer should be steady, not merely emotional. We do not pray only when we feel strong. We pray because the Lord is worthy of our trust in every season. And as we bring our requests before Him, we also learn to rest in His wisdom.
