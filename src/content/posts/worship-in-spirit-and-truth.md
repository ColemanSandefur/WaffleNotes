# Worship in Spirit and Truth

## Giving God the honor He deserves

Worship is one of the most important practices of the Christian life, and yet it is often misunderstood. Some think of worship as a matter of preference, while others treat it as a routine. In truth, worship is the deliberate offering of our hearts to God in a way that honors Him and reflects His truth.

> "God is spirit, and those who worship him must worship in spirit and truth." <cite>John 4:24</cite>

Jesus spoke of worshiping the Father in spirit and truth. This means worship is not merely external. It must come from a sincere heart, but it is also shaped by truth. We cannot worship God rightly if we ignore His Word or replace His instructions with our own preferences. True worship is both heartfelt and faithful.

The worship of the church is therefore not casual. It is serious, reverent, and intentional. It includes prayer, singing, teaching, and the remembrance of the Lord’s Supper. These practices are not empty traditions. They are ways by which the people of God gather around the Lord and express their devotion to Him.

### A worshipful life includes

- Reverent prayer
- Faithful obedience
- grateful singing
- steady remembrance of the Lord

When worship is done well, it strengthens the community of faith. It unites believers around a common purpose and reminds them of the Lord’s presence. It turns their attention away from self and toward God. In that sense, worship is not only a private act; it is also a public testimony.

The church must be careful not to let worship become reduced to entertainment or habit. Worship should never be shaped only by what feels good to the crowd. It should be shaped by reverence for God and faithfulness to His Word. When that happens, worship becomes a source of strength and not simply a schedule to be kept.

May we approach worship with sincerity, humility, and joy. The Lord deserves our best, and He invites us to give Him our hearts in worship.
