# Learning to Listen

## A habit that shapes the heart

Listening is a discipline that many people neglect. We often speak quickly, respond quickly, and assume quickly. Yet listening well is a form of humility. It allows us to understand others more clearly and to hear from God with greater attentiveness.

> "Let every person be quick to hear, slow to speak, slow to anger." <cite>James 1:19</cite>

When we learn to listen, we become less defensive and more thoughtful. We are better able to grasp the concerns of others, and we become more ready to receive wisdom from the Lord. Listening does not mean we agree with everything, but it does mean we take time to understand.

In spiritual life, listening is essential. We must listen to Scripture, to the prompting of the Spirit, and to the needs of those around us. A believer who listens well is often growing in wisdom, patience, and compassion.
